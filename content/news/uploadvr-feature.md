We're thrilled to announce that **PiEEG XR** has been featured on **UploadVR**, one of the leading voices in virtual reality news and coverage!

## Making Avatars Expressive Without Face Tracking

In their in-depth article "[This Quest 3 Accessory Makes Your VR Avatar Expressive Without Face Tracking](https://www.uploadvr.com/pieeg-xr-makes-your-vr-avatar-expressive-without-face-tracking/)," UploadVR explores how PiEEG XR represents a different approach to avatar expression—one that reads biological signals instead of relying on cameras.

### What Makes It Different

As UploadVR highlights, PiEEG XR is designed as an open-source neural face interface for spatial computing. The article showcases our smile detection demo, where the system learns to recognize facial expressions through biosignal training and maps them to VR avatar expressions in real-time.

### Beyond Simple Smiles

The coverage also explores the broader potential of PiEEG XR—from controlling additional virtual limbs to mapping focus states and other biosignals into mixed-reality effects. As developer Ildar Rakhmatulin shared with UploadVR, PiEEG XR is meant to enable exactly these kinds of experiments.

### A Developer-First Approach

UploadVR correctly positions PiEEG XR as a hackable kit for developers, researchers, and VRChat experimenters—not a plug-and-play consumer product, but a platform for exploring what's possible when you combine biosignal sensing with spatial computing.
